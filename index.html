Research

What I Study

My research explores the radio universe from multiple angles, tracing giant radio jets across megaparsecs, studying AGN feedback in galaxy clusters, and building machine-learning pipelines to classify complex radio morphologies at scale.

Radio-loud AGN & Environment

Studying how radio-loud active galactic nuclei interact with their surrounding medium in dense and merging galaxy clusters, using uGMRT, VLA, and large survey data.

Giant Radio Galaxies & Quasars

Discovering and characterising giant radio sources (GRGs and GRQs) from surveys like TGSS ADR1, exploring their radio, optical, infrared, and environmental properties.

Hybrid Morphology Radio Sources

Investigating HYMORS, radio sources with FR I jets on one side and FR II on the other, from the MeerKAT Absorption Line Survey, examining their mid-infrared and environmental characteristics.

ML Morphological Classification

Building automated pipelines to detect and classify complex radio galaxy morphologies from large radio sky surveys.

Disk–Jet Connection

Exploring how accretion processes in supermassive black holes drive relativistic jet formation and the observed asymmetry in extragalactic radio sources.

Multiwavelength Observations

Combining radio, X-ray, optical, and infrared data to build comprehensive pictures of radio source populations across cosmic time.
