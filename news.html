New paper accepted in MNRAS: Bow-and-Arrow Radio Galaxy in a Multi-Halo Environment
A Rad@home discovery paper on a ~560 kpc bow-shock structure in a multi-halo environment has been accepted for publication in Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society. I am a co-author on this work alongside Hota, Dabhade, Ghosh, Pal, and others.

Our study on hybrid morphology radio sources from the MeerKAT Absorption Line Survey — characterising their radio, mid-infrared, and environmental properties — has been published in ApJ 997(2), 157.

Research covered internationally: "Scientists discover 53 powerful quasars with jets up to 50× the Milky Way"
Our giant radio quasar study was featured in Space.com and other international media outlets, bringing attention to the discovery of 53 powerful quasars with radio jets spanning up to 50 times the size of the Milky Way.

2025 Publication
Two new papers published: Giant Radio Quasars (ApJS) and Circular Mystery (ApJ)
Published two new journal articles: one unveiling 53 new giant radio quasars from the TGSS sky (ApJS 281, 34) and another exploring diffuse emission around a radio galaxy using uGMRT and VLA (ApJ 987, 10).
